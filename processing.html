Our WOOL PROCESSING

RAW WOOL PROCUREMENT

Wool processing begins with the procurement of raw wool. This can be sourced from local farms, wool auctions, or international suppliers. The quality and type of wool obtained can vary based on the intended end products.

SHEARING

Raw wool is obtained through shearing, which involves removing the fleece from sheep. Shearing is typically performed by trained shearers to ensure the welfare of the animals and the quality of the wool.

SKIRTING AND SORTING

After shearing, the raw wool is skirted and sorted. Skirting involves removing undesirable portions of the fleece, such as stained or coarse wool. Sorting categorizes the wool based on factors like fiber diameter, color, and cleanliness.

WASHING AND SCOURING

Raw wool contains natural oils (lanolin), dirt, and impurities. It is washed or scoured in hot water with mild detergents to remove these substances. Thorough cleaning is crucial to producing high-quality wool.

CARDING

The cleaned wool is carded to separate and align the individual fibers. Carding machines have wire brushes or teeth that accomplish this task, creating a web of parallel fibers.

SPINNING

Carded wool is spun into yarn using either traditional methods or modern spinning machinery. The thickness and texture of the yarn can be adjusted during this process.

DYEING

If colored wool is desired, it can be dyed using various dyeing techniques at this stage.

WEAVING OR KNITTING

The spun yarn is used to create fabrics through weaving or knitting. Weaving involves interlacing yarns on a loom to create fabric, while knitting forms fabric by interlocking loops of yarn.

FINISHING

Finished fabric may go through additional processes like steaming, pressing, or brushing to improve its texture, appearance, and durability.

CUT ANF SEW

If the final product is clothing, the fabric is cut into pattern pieces and sewn together to create garments. This involves stitching, hemming, and other sewing techniques.

QUALITY CONTROL

Throughout the processing stages, quality control measures are implemented to ensure the final products meet desired standards in terms of texture, color, strength, and other factors.

PACKAGING AND DISTRIBUTION

Processed woolen products are packaged and prepared for distribution to wholesalers, retailers, or directly to consumers through various marketing channels.
